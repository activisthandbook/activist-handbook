If you want to achieve social change, it is advisable to think about how you are going to organise yourself in the long term. In many cases, you will not achieve your goal after a single protest. Sometimes movements grow very fast and lose focus. Setting up a proper organisation will help you to work more effectively and prevent future problems.

You can contribute: This handbook is written by people like you. Feel free to create an account and start editing today! Find out more about how to contribute.

Organising

  1. Set up an organisation: Learn about building an organisation or branch.
  2. Power: Learn about power structures (both visible and invisible), and how your organisation can improve decision-making and empower its members. 
  3. Internal communication: Learn how to communicate with your organisation's members.
  4. Finance: Learn how your organisation can raise money and manage it better.
  5. Diversity: Explore how to tackle structural oppression and inaccessibility in your organisation.
  6. Mobilising, recruitment & engagement: Learn how to increase the number of people in your organisation, through activating supporters (recruitment), activating non-supporters (mobilisation), and keeping your activists active (engagement). 
  7. Knowledge: Learn how to grow knowledge in your members (training), grow the knowledge in your organisation (organisational learning), and manage that knowledge in a systemic way (knowledge management).
