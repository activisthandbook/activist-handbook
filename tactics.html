Here is a list of various tactics that activists use in their work. Note that you do not have to choose one particular tactic. The most successful movements use a combination of tactics and allow people to contribute to a cause in their own way. 

The tactics are categorised to make it easier for you to find the right one, but tactics may actually fall under multiple categories at the same time or none at all.

Many of the tactics below require you to assemble many people. Make sure to check out our chapter about collective action to find out more about this topic.

You can contribute: This handbook is written by people like you. Feel free to add tactics you know or suggest a different way of categorising! Use this tactic page template. Find out more about how to contribute.

Choosing your tactic

The following section was based on the chapter 'Using your power' in Youth Activist Toolkit, by Advocates for Youth (2019)

Tactics are actions taken to get someone (your target) to give you what you want (your demands). Each tactic should fit within your overall strategy to reach your end goal. There are a variety of tactics that you can choose from––your goal is to determine which tactics to use and when in order to get your group what it wants.

Here are some basic guidelines for selecting tactics:

  1. They should show support for your demands.
  2. They should be focused on your primary or secondary target.
  3. They should demonstrate your power, while simultaneously building your power.
  4. They should be outside the comfort zone of your target and within the comfort zone of your group

Protests

Protesting is great way to attract the attention of the public, either by gathering a large crowd or by doing something out of the ordinary.

Topics: March | Flash mob | Occupation | Direct action | Human banner | Cacerolazo | Hunger strike

Creative

Activism can be beautiful. By using creative tactics you can make your movement stand out from the crowd.

Topics: Posters, stickers and stamps | Film and photography | Art | Theatre | Music

Digital

There are many ways of digital activism. Some of them include sharing pictures and stories, building a network or sharing a certain hashtag, getting as many people as possible to sign a petition.

  • Social media
  • Petition
  • Hacktivism

Conversational

Sometimes having a conversation is better than shouting slogans. 

  • Education
  • Canvassing (going door-to-door, handing out flyers)
  • Hosting a talk or debate

Storytelling

It takes courage to speak up. Storytelling can bring important issues to the attention of the public. 

  • Journalism
  • Whistleblowing
  • Blogging
  • Novels

Institutional

By joining existing institutions one can make change happen from within.

  • Political actions: call to vote | becoming electable
  • Organisation: joining boards | setting up new working groups

Financial

  • Boycot
  • Divestment
