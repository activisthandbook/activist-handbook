Here is a list of various tactics that activists use in their work. Note that you do not have to choose one particular tactic. The most successful movements use a combination of tactics and allow people to contribute to a cause in their own way. 

The tactics are categorised to make it easier for you to find the right one, but tactics may actually fall under multiple categories at the same time or none at all. Feel free to add tactics you know or suggest a different way of categorising! Use this tactic page template.

Many of the tactics below require you to assemble many people. Make sure to check out our chapter about collective change to find out more about this topic.

Protests

Protesting is great way to attract the attention of the public, either by gathering a large crowd or by doing something out of the ordinary.

  • March
  • Flash mob
  • Occupation (eg. sit-in, die-in)
  • Direct action (eg. civil disobedience)
  • Human banner
  • Cacerolazo (making a lot of noise)
  • Hunger strike

Creative

Activism can be beautiful. By using creative tactics you can make your movement stand out from the crowd.

  • Posters, stickers and stamps
  • Film and photography
  • Art
  • Theatre
  • Music

Starting a conversation

Sometimes having a conversation is better than shouting slogans. 

  • Education
  • Door-to-door
  • Hosting a talk or debate

Digital

There are many ways of digital activism. Some of them include sharing pictures and stories, building a network or sharing a certain hashtag, getting as many people as possible to sign a petition.

  • Social media
  • Petition
  • Hacktivism

Financial

  • Boycot
  • Divestment

Storytelling

It takes courage to speak up. Storytelling can bring important issues to the attention of the public. 

  • Journalism
  • Whistleblowing
  • Blogging
  • Novels
