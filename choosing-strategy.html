Choosing your tactics

“In the world of give and take, tactics is the art of how to take and how to give…. our concern is… how the Have-nots can take power away from the Haves”

- Saul Alinsky (Rules for Radicals, p.92)

This section was based on the chapter 'Using your power' in Youth Activist Toolkit, by Advocates for Youth (2019)

Here are some quick steps to help you decide what tactics to use: 

  1. Make clear what change you would like to see. You are calling upon others to do something, so make sure they know what exactly you want.
  2. Get the attention of your targets. Who are affected? Who might support your cause? Who has to power to decide on the issue?
  3. Use your power and act collectively. Whatever you do, do it together with others. The harsh truth: you will probably not achieve your goal after a single action, so make sure to build your power every time.
  4. Allow people to contribute in their own way. Enable supporters to contribute to your cause in whatever way they feel comfortable. You might be willing to chain yourself to a tree to save it, but others might prefer to organise a theatre play to create more awareness about the importance of not cutting trees.
